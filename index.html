About me

I am currently a second year student at Miami University in Oxford, Ohio. I plan to graduate in 2019 and seek graduate education in Statistics. I am a Mathematics and Statistics major with a co-major in Predictive Analytics and minors in Computer Science and Operations Research Methods. This summer I have accepted a position with IBM as an Information Technology Intern in Research Triagle Park, NC.

During my time at Miami, I have been busy immersing myself in the Statistics Department and Center for Analytics and Data Science. Some of my accomplishments so far have been participating in the Government Section for Statistics 2016 Data Challenge, where my team worked for a semester to scrape, wrangle and visualize data for car accidents and car safety ratings. See our paper and R Shiny App from the 2016 GSS Data Challenge. I also participated in Miami's first annual DataFest, where my team of first-year classmates won Best in Show. See a picture of the team up top and see our findings and data visualizations. I'm currently working with a new team for the 2017 GSS Data Challenge. We are looking to incorporate a SQL server into our project to manage over 15 GB of data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics Condumer Expenditure survey. I will be traveling to Baltimore in July to present our findings at the Joint Statistical Meetings.
